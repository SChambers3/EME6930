Subject

The subject is a noun or pronoun that performs the action verb or is subject to the linking verb.


Verb

There are three main types of verbs: action verbs, helping verbs, and linking verbs.

Action verbs show what the subject is doing. Helping verbs help to show the tense of the verb. Linking verbs connect the subject to a predicate nominative or predicate adjective.


Modifer

Modifiers are words that change, clarify, qualify, limit, or modify other words. Articles, adjectives, adverbs, and prepositional phrases all act as Modifiers.


Object of a Preposition

The Object of Preposition is the noun or pronoun that follows a preposition to complete its meaning.


Direct Object

A Direct Object is the noun or pronoun that is recipient of the action of a transitive verb.


Indirect Object

An Indirect Object is the noun or pronoun that is affected by the action of a transitive verb (usually the recipient) but not the object.


Predicate Nominative

A Predicate Nominative is the nominative noun used after a linking verb to complement the subject.


Predicate Adjective

A Predicate Adjective is the adjective used after a linking verb to modify the subject.
