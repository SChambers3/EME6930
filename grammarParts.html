Nouns

Nouns are naming words for people, places, things, and ideas. Nouns are grouped into common and proper nouns.


Articles and Adjectives

There are three articles in the English language. Two indefinite articles "a" and "an" and the definite article "the".

An adjective is a descriptive word that modifies a noun or pronoun.


Pronouns

A pronoun takes the place of a noun, or it may replace a person's name.


Prepositions

A preposition is used with a noun or a pronoun to make a prepositional phrase.

Prepositional phrases refer to position, time, or movement.


Subjects and Verbs

Every sentence must have a subject (noun/pronoun) and a verb.

There are three main verb types: action verbs, linking verbs, and helping verbs.


Adverbs

An adverb is a descriptive word that modifies an adjective, a verb, or another adverb.

Adverbs tell how, when, where, or to what extent.


Interjections

An interjection is a abrupt remark that shows strong feeling or reaction.


Conjunctions

A conjunction is a word that join groups of similar words together.

The most common conjunctions in English are: and, but, or, nor, for, and yet.
